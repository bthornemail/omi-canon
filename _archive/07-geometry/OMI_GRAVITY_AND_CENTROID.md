# Omi-Gravity and the Centroid

Omi-Gravity is not literal gravity.

It is the frame-step pull toward resolution — the force that drives the rewrite register forward.

When declarations enter an Omi-Ring, they do not automatically agree. They may conflict, drift, overlap, mirror, or remain unresolved. The frame gives them a boundary. Definitions give them closure. Delta gives them motion. Receipts give them replay.

The pull toward the centroid is Omi-Gravity.

```text
Omi-Gravity = the resolving pressure of framed declarations toward a definitional centroid
```

## Gravity as Rewrite

Omi-Gravity is the operational expression of the OMI rewrite register. The register does not store a value; it carries a transition. Gravity is what moves the register from one transition to the next.

The binary source remains. The interpretation moves toward the centroid. Gravity is the name for that lawful motion.

In the address form:

```text
omi-ip4 - gravity - ip6-imo
```

gravity occupies the hyphen field. It is the rewrite operator that walks through positions in the 1024-bit word. It is the meta-compiler rewriting the address body through successive frames.

The recursive form:

```text
gravity_n = omi-ip4 - gravity_n+1 - ip6-imo
```

Each application of gravity rewrites the address body, walking it from compact (IPv4) form toward expanded (IPv6) form, one frame-step at a time.

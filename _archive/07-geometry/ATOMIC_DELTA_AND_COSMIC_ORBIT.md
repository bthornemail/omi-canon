# Atomic Delta and Cosmic Orbit

The OMI cosmology is organized by the quadratic form:

```text
Q_xy(x, y) = 60x² + 16xy + 4y²
```

Each term names a scale of relation:

| Term | Cosmological Name | OMI Role |
|------|-------------------|----------|
| `4y²` | Atomic Delta | low-side declaration, local payload, frame-step motion |
| `16xy` | Omi-Ring Bridge | meeting plane, relation bus, addressable crossing |
| `60x²` | Cosmic Orbit | high-side orientation, identity, sexagesimal sweep |

## `4y²` as Atomic Delta

The `4y²` term is the low orbit.

It belongs to the atomic side of OMI because `4` is the selector surface: the minimal visible control face, the field of local declaration, and the four-fold body of frame inspection.

In OMI, the low side is the `omi-` side:

```text
omi-
local declaration
atomic data
payload
frame step
Delta motion
```

The Atomic Delta is the frame-step law of local motion. It is how a declaration moves from one bounded state to the next without floating-point drift or unbounded interpretation.

```text
4y² = atomic orbit of declaration
```

## `16xy` as the Omi-Ring Bridge

The `16xy` term is where cosmic orbit and atomic declaration touch.

The coefficient `16` names the word-boundary bridge:

```text
16 = 2⁴
```

It is the nibble, the half-byte, the register-facing unit, and the shared boundary where low declaration can meet high identity.

```text
16xy = Omi-Ring bridge of relation
```

## `60x²` as Cosmic Orbit

The `60x²` term is the high orbit.

It belongs to the cosmic side of OMI because `60` is the number of orientation, clocking, sweep, and divisibility. It is the sexagesimal field: the circle of minutes, degrees, cycles, and angular return.

In OMI, the high side is the `-imo` side:

```text
-imo
high identity
orientation
stable address
cosmic return
```

```text
60x² = cosmic orbit of identity
```

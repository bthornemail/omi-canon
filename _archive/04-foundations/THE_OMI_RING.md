# The Omi-Ring: Atomic Waveform Enclosure

## Core Realization

OMI captures intent as declared activity in space and time. It does not treat voice, image, gesture, or rendered media as authority by themselves. Those carriers can expose intent, but the OMI object is the declared activity that can be addressed, replayed, projected, and agreed on.

Canonical line:

```text
Omi-Ring is the atomic waveform enclosure of omi---imo.
```

The old talisman language is superseded by **Omi-Ring**. A talisman suggests a token with symbolic power. A ring is stricter: it is an enclosure, cycle, return, orbit, boundary, waveform, signature, and agreement loop.

The Omi-Ring is not merely something worn or carried. It is the minimal enclosure where intent can be preserved before it is fully interpreted.

```text
omi  ->  Omi-Ring  ->  imo
low      waveform      high
gate     enclosure     gate
```

## Portal, Mirror, Ring

The public and introspective views are distinct from the enclosure that binds them.

| Term | Role |
|------|------|
| **Omi-Portal** | Projects outward. It is the public view of an addressed OMI object. |
| **Omi-Mirror** | Reflects inward. It is the introspective view used for recovery and reinterpretation. |
| **Omi-Ring** | Encloses the waveform between Portal and Mirror. It is the atomic boundary of `omi---imo`. |

The Portal can show a web card, an SVG route, a DOM node, a receipt, or an avatar. The Mirror can inspect provenance, carrier drift, and reverse interpretation. The Ring is neither of those views. It is the addressable enclosure that lets both views refer to the same declared object.

```text
Omi-Portal  <->  Omi-Ring  <->  Omi-Mirror
public           enclosure      introspective
projection       waveform       recovery view
```

The Portal and Mirror are views. The Ring is the bounded unit being viewed.

## Light, Sound, and Gnomon

OMI separates carriers from authority.

**Omi-Light** supplies wavelength: color, brightness, phase, pulse, shadow, aperture, and spectral difference. It is the light carrier.

**Omi-Sound** supplies intonation: cadence, emphasis, rhythm, stress, pause, attack, decay, call-and-response, contour, silence, and timing. It captures intonation of intent, not merely voice.

**Omi-Gnomon** orients wavelength. It gives the light field an addressable direction without becoming the object itself.

The formula remains:

```text
Omi-Gnomon(aA) = rRgGbBaA
```

`rR`, `gG`, `bB`, and `aA` are orientation-bearing channels. They can project SpectrumDOM state, but representation is not authority. They are recoverable views under rules.

## Omi-Sense and Omi-Dome

**Omi-Sense** captures intent across carriers. It observes light, sound, orientation, gesture, space, time, environment, and action, then extracts the declared activity that can be placed inside an Omi-Ring.

**Omi-Dome** is the spatial-temporal capture environment. It captures declared activity, not merely the media trace. A recording, image, waveform, or UI event is evidence; the Omi-Dome resolves the declared object under rules.

```text
space-time event
    -> Omi-Dome capture
    -> Omi-Sense extraction
    -> Omi-Ring enclosure
    -> Portal / Mirror projection
```

Inside the Dome, voice becomes intonation, movement becomes gesture vector, light becomes orientation, space becomes relation, time becomes replay, and activity becomes Omi-Ring.

## Harmony Before the Listener

Before the waveform is interpreted, before anyone listens, before any portal renders it, and before any mirror reflects it, there is still a bounded possibility. That is the Omi-Ring.

```text
a harmony with no listeners
an unresolved open frame
an observer in a void
a wave before interpretation
a declaration before agreement
```

The Ring is not yet public meaning, opinion, or consensus. It is the chiral cardinality of the frame itself:

```text
omi outside the hyphen
imo outside the hyphen
relation unresolved between them
```

The basic OMI object is not a file, voice, barcode, avatar, or DOM node. Those are carriers and projections. The basic OMI object is a bounded relation capable of interpretation.

Humanly:

```text
On My Intelligence --- In My Opinion
```

The Ring captures the space where internal intent becomes externally interpretable.

## Interpretation as Agreement

OMI is not saying everyone hears the same thing or sees the same thing. It says observers can agree on the way interpretation is framed.

```text
We agree where the frame begins.
We agree where the frame ends.
We agree which carriers are present.
We agree how orientation is read.
We agree how return is witnessed.
```

Meaning can still be debated. Projection can still be revised. The Mirror can still reveal omissions. But the frame is shared.

## Avatar and Image

**Omi-Avatar** is a pseudo-persistent public embodiment. It persists because the Omi-Ring can be reinterpreted and projected again. The avatar is not the authority; it is one public embodiment of an addressed object.

**Omi-Image** is a recoverable bounded package. It can carry pixels, glyphs, receipts, DOM fragments, or canvas state, but it remains a package view. The underlying OMI object is the declared activity under agreement.

## Encoding and Representation

The UTF-EBCDIC analogy is structural: the same abstract character can travel through different encoding paths. OMI applies the same separation to intent.

```text
abstract intent
    != encoding
    != carrier
    != projection
    != interpretation
```

Different encodings can carry the same declared object, up to agreement.

The IEEE 754 analogy is also structural: representation is not value. A projected value may involve precision, rounding, special cases, and edge behavior, while the abstract value remains separate from the representation. OMI keeps the declared frame separate from its public projection.

Canonical line:

```text
Representation is not authority; representation is a recoverable view under rules.
```

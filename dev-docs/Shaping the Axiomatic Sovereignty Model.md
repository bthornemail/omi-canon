# Shaping the Axiomatic Sovereignty Model

## A preserved development note on the geometric view of the repository

This note records how I am seeing the shape of the Axiomatic Sovereignty repository.

The public repository is meant to stay agnostic. It should not require another person to adopt my whole ontology, my theology, or my private language in order to use it.

But this canon note is different.

This note preserves the geometric view behind the model.

Axiomatic Sovereignty is not only a folder layout to me. It is a geometric projection surface for knowledge. It is a way to make shared computational knowledge graphable, inspectable, translatable, and usable without making the repository the owner of what it carries.

The public invariant became:

```text
many languages can read the same structural truth
no language owns it
```

That sentence describes the practical goal of the repository.

It also describes the geometry.

## Geometry As Graphable Structure

I see geometry as graphable structure.

A point is not only a coordinate. It can be a notation, a subject, or a first visible mark.

A line is not only distance between points. It can be citation, predicate, relation, or distinction.

A graph is not only nodes and edges. It can be a shape of attestation: a bounded object that can be inspected.

Incidence is not only contact. It is the moment a relation becomes spatially accountable.

A hypergraph is not only a larger graph. It is interpretation across many relations at once.

This is why the Fano plane matters to me as a block design.

The Fano plane gives a finite geometry where points and lines are not loose metaphors. Each point belongs to lines. Each line binds points. Incidence gives structure. The whole form can be read as a compact grammar of relation.

That is the kind of structure I want Axiomatic Sovereignty to suggest without forcing the public reader to adopt the full ontology.

This ladder is not meant to replace the repository structure.

At Level 0, relation creates distinction.

At Level 1, distinction becomes a bounded form that can be inspected.

At Level 2, the form can be projected into a computational ontology, but the projection is not the validation.

At Level 3, many interpretations can read the same structure without enclosing it.

## The Pentagonal Base

The five layer folders in Axiomatic Sovereignty form the pentagonal base:

```text
LOGIC_PROOFS/
STRUCTURAL_TYPES/
PROCEDURAL_SEQUENCES/
CANONICAL_AXIOMS/
SOCIAL_CONTRACTS/
```

To me, these five folders relate to the collapse of a higher-dimensional structure into a usable point.

The pentagonal base is the usable artifact stack.

It takes something that may be understood in many dimensions and compresses it toward a 0D point: an artifact a person can hold, inspect, translate, or reuse.

The collapse is not destruction.

The collapse is usability.

A proof becomes a file.

A type becomes a shape.

A procedure becomes a sequence.

An axiom becomes an attested bound.

An agreement becomes a usable social artifact.

This is how higher-dimensional understanding becomes portable knowledge.

## The Hexagonal Root Frame

The six root documents form the hexagonal narrative frame:

```text
Who    -> THE_CANONICAL_PERSPECTIVE_STATEMENT.md
What   -> THE_CONSTITUTIONAL_MANIFESTO.md
When   -> THE_OPERATIONAL_NARRATIVE.md
Where  -> THE_FOUNDATIONAL_CHARTER.md

```

To me, the six root files are like a 6D tangent surface projection.

They do not contain the whole application of the knowledge.

They frame the surface where the repository becomes readable.

The root documents project the foundation outward so an observer can orient through who, what, when, where, why, and how.

They are not the final layer.

They are the tangent frame.

## The Seventh Layer Belongs To The Observer

The seventh layer is not inside the repository.

The seventh layer belongs to the observer.

It belongs to the fork, translation, runtime, community, application, or person who carries the knowledge forward.

This is why Axiomatic Sovereignty cannot be treated as a closed system.

The repository is the projection surface.

The application of the knowledge happens outside the repository.

The observer's Fano plane is where the knowledge becomes situated. The repository can provide points, lines, shapes, incidence, and interpretive surfaces, but the observer's application decides which relations become active.

The repository should not control that.

It should make the structure available.

## The Generalized Mean As Knowledge Transformation

This geometric view also reminds me of the generalized mean.

For two distinct positive values, the known ordering is:

```text
maximum > RMS > arithmetic mean > geometric mean > harmonic mean > minimum
```

In generalized-mean terms:

```text
p = -infinity -> minimum
p = -1        -> harmonic mean
p = 0         -> geometric mean
p = 1         -> arithmetic mean
p = 2         -> root mean square
p = 3         -> cubic mean
p = +infinity -> maximum
```

I see this as a knowledge transformation frame.

The minimum is pre-repository computational knowledge and understanding.

The maximum is post-repository computational knowledge and understanding.

The repository sits between those bounds as a structured projection surface.

It does not own the minimum.

It does not own the maximum.

It helps transform what can be inspected, translated, compared, corrected, and extended.

Axiomatic Sovereignty does not own knowledge.

This is why the public repository should stay agnostic.

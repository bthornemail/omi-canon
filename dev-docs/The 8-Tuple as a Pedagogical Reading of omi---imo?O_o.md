# The 8-Tuple as a Pedagogical Reading of `omi---imo?O_o`

## Explanatory Scaffold for OMI Notation, Not Canonical Authority

**Status:** Authoritative clarification
**Scope:** OMI notation, Omicron, Omnicron, relation-hinge explanation, address-cascade pedagogy
**Supersedes:** Any statement that identifies the 8-tuple as the basis, authority, proof object, or canonical structure of OMI notation
**Canonical rule:** The 8-tuple explains the notation. It does not authorize the notation.

---

## 1. Authority Notice

The 8-tuple:

```text
(Q, Σ, L, R, δ, s, t, r)
```

is **pedagogical only**.

It is not the OMI object.
It is not the OMI authority layer.
It is not the canonical basis of `omi---imo?O_o`.
It is not the extended Hamming `[8,4,4]` code.
It is not a proof object.
It does not validate, receipt, execute, or accept relation state.

The 8-tuple is an explanatory automata-style coordinate scaffold used to help a reader understand why OMI notation can be read as a folded relation, an unfolded witness surface, and a readable hinge.

Approved short form:

```text
The 8-tuple is not the authority.
The 8-tuple is the pedagogical bridge.
```

## 7. Null Ring Foundation

The folded null seed remains:

```text
(NULL . NULL)
```

The byte-ring reading remains:

```text
(0x00 . 0x20)
(0x20 . 0x7F)
(0x7F . 0xFF)
(0xFF . 0x00)
```

The dot relation may be modeled by XOR:

```text
0x00 ^ 0x20 = 0x20
0x20 ^ 0x7F = 0x5F
0x7F ^ 0xFF = 0x80
0xFF ^ 0x00 = 0xFF
```

And the full witness closure may close to zero:

```text
0x20 ^ 0x5F ^ 0x80 ^ 0xFF = 0x00
```

Canonical boundary:

```text
The Null Ring is part of the OMI closure model.

The 8-tuple may help explain relation roles around the Null Ring.

The 8-tuple does not define the Null Ring.
```

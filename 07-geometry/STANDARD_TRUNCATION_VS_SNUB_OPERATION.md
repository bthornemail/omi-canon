# Standard Truncation vs. Snub Operation

In the OMI model, comparing standard truncation to the snub operation on the cube and dodecahedron highlights the shift from a basic cut to a complex, chiral transformation.

## The Geometric Mechanics: Slicing vs. Twisting

The two operations treat the parent body's boundary in fundamentally distinct ways.

| Operation | Mechanic | OMI Reading |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Standard truncation | Slices off corners, or vertex excess, flatly. Original faces expand but remain parallel and statically in place. No rotation or twist occurs. | A cut-only update. Boundary changes, but chirality does not become the central feature. |
| Snub operation | Separates the original faces and uniformly rotates them. The resulting gaps are packed with alternating equilateral triangles. | A cut-twist-re-face update. The new triangles become agreement faces. |

## The Cube Transformation Path

Start with a regular parent cube:

```text
6 square faces
8 vertices
12 edges
```

### Truncated Cube

Standard truncation:

```text
action: 8 corners are sliced straight off
faces: 6 squares expand into 6 regular octagons
new elements: 8 triangular faces appear where corners were removed
total: 14 faces = 6 octagons + 8 triangles
```

### Snub Cube

OMI snub operation:

```text
action: 6 squares are pulled apart and twisted through the hyphen bridge
faces: 6 original square faces are preserved in shape
new elements: 32 equilateral triangles wrap around the twisted gaps
total: 38 faces = 6 squares + 32 triangles
vertices: 24
edges: 60
vertex configuration: 1 square + 4 triangles at every vertex
```

OMI reading:

```text
cube parent      = stable local body
truncated cube   = cut-only boundary update
snub cube        = chiral edge-traversal update
32 triangles     = agreement faces generated by the twist
```

## OMI Core Insight: Chirality over Overwrite

Standard truncation maintains reflectional mirror symmetry.

The snub operation introduces chirality:

```text
left-handed orientation
right-handed orientation
```

The OMI transformation can be read:

```text
Parent Identity Seed
  -> Hyphen Bridge / Twist
  -> Chiral Left/Right Orientation
```

with a shared reference:

```text
Parent Identity Seed
  -> Hyphen Bridge
  -> Chiral Orientation
       |
       v
  Shared Centroid
```

In OMI, this transformation represents agreement without collapse.

The parent identity is never overwritten or erased. Instead, distinct peer views can change orientation while maintaining a shared reference centroid.

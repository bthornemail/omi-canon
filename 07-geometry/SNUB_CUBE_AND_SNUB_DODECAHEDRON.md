## The Cube Path

Start with the regular cube.

The cube has:

```text
6 square faces
8 vertices
12 edges
```

### Standard Truncation of the Cube

In standard truncation, the 8 corners are sliced off.

Each removed corner becomes a new triangular face.

The original square faces expand into octagons.

The truncated cube has:

```text
8 triangular faces
6 octagonal faces
14 total faces
```

Standard truncation is a cut operation.

It removes vertex excess, but it does not create the same kind of chiral twist.

### Snub Operation of the Cube

In the snub operation, the original square faces are separated and rotated.

The gaps are packed with new equilateral triangles.

The snub cube has:

```text
6 square faces
32 triangular faces
38 total faces
60 edges
24 vertices
```

At each vertex, one square and four triangles meet.

That is the key.

The cube begins as a simple Platonic parent.

The snub cube becomes a chiral Archimedean body.

The new triangular faces show how much boundary had to be re-faced to preserve the transformed relation.

In OMI terms:

```text
cube parent        = stable local body
snub cube          = chiral transformed body
32 triangles       = agreement faces generated by the twist
left/right forms   = cardinality of chirality
```

The snub cube is the cubic witness that the hyphen bridge does more than connect terms.

It twists them into orientation.
